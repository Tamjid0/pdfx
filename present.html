Presentation Notes & Speaker Guide

Slide 1 of 6
Title Slide: Introduction

What to Say (Script)

"Good morning everyone! Today we're going to explore a critical question: How do we build trust in AI chatbots?"

"As AI becomes more integrated into our daily lives, understanding user trust isn't just important—it's essential for adoption."

Duration: approximately 30 seconds

What to Do (Delivery Notes)

  • Make eye contact with the audience
  • Smile and speak with energy
  • Pause after the key question for emphasis
  • Use hand gesture when saying "integrated into our daily lives"

Deep Dive (Safety Net & Background Information)

If asked: "Why is trust important?"
Trust determines adoption rates. Studies show 67% of users abandon AI tools they don't trust, regardless of functionality. Trust equals retention.
Background context:
The global AI chatbot market is expected to reach $15.5 billion by 2028, but trust remains the number one barrier to adoption according to recent surveys.
Slide 2 of 6
The Trust Problem

What to Say (Script)

"Let's start with the problem. Only 35% of users fully trust AI chatbots with their information."

"The main concerns? Privacy, accuracy, and transparency. Users want to know: Is my data safe? Will I get correct information? And how does this thing actually work?"

Duration: approximately 45 seconds

What to Do (Delivery Notes)

  • Look concerned when stating the 35% statistic
  • Count on fingers: "Privacy, accuracy, transparency"
  • Raise eyebrows on each question to engage audience
  • Scan the room to gauge reactions

Deep Dive (Safety Net & Background Information)

Source for 35% statistic:
Pew Research Center, 2024 AI Trust Survey (Sample size: 5,000 users across demographics)
If asked: "What about different age groups?"
Ages 18-29: 42% trust; Ages 30-49: 35% trust; Ages 50+: 28% trust. Younger users are more trusting but still cautious.
Additional concerns mentioned by users:
  • Bias in responses (mentioned by 52%)
  • Job displacement fears (38%)
  • Inability to understand context (44%)
Slide 3 of 6
Four Key Trust Factors

What to Say (Script)

"So what builds trust? We've identified four key factors."

"First, transparency in how the AI works. Second, consistent and accurate responses. Third, clear data handling policies. And fourth, human-like but honest interaction."

"Notice I said 'human-like but honest'—users don't want to be fooled."

Duration: approximately 50 seconds

What to Do (Delivery Notes)

  • Hold up four fingers when mentioning "four key factors"
  • Point to slide for each factor as you mention it
  • Emphasize "honest" with vocal stress
  • Make direct eye contact on "don't want to be fooled"

Deep Dive (Safety Net & Background Information)

Factor rankings by importance:
1. Accuracy (78% rated as critical)
2. Data privacy (72%)
3. Transparency (65%)
4. Natural interaction (51%)
Example of "honest interaction":
ChatGPT saying "I don't know" or "I may be wrong" increases trust by 23% compared to always giving confident answers (Stanford study, 2024).
Transparency examples:
  • Showing sources for information
  • Explaining limitations upfront
  • Disclosing when AI isn't certain

Data sources:
Meta-analysis of 47 AI chatbot platforms, 2023-2024. Sample size: 2.3 million users. Conducted by MIT's AI Lab.
If asked: "Which industries see the biggest impact?"
Healthcare (+78%), Banking (+71%), E-commerce (+58%). Healthcare sees highest impact due to sensitivity of information.
Return on investment perspective:
Companies implementing trust-building features see average ROI of 340% within 18 months through reduced churn and support costs.
Slide 5 of 6
Practical Solutions

What to Say (Script)

"So how do we actually implement this? Three practical solutions:"

"One: Design with transparency in mind from day one. Two: Regular accuracy audits and user feedback loops. Three: Clear communication about what the AI can and cannot do."

"It's not rocket science, but it requires commitment."

Duration: approximately 45 seconds

What to Do (Delivery Notes)

  • Move to center stage for this slide
  • Hold up three fingers for "three solutions"
  • Use hand gestures for each point
  • Smile slightly on "not rocket science"
  • Look serious on "requires commitment"

Deep Dive (Safety Net & Background Information)

Implementation timeline:
Transparency features: 2-3 months. Audit systems: 1-2 months. Clear communication framework: 2-4 weeks. Total: approximately 4-6 months for full implementation.
Real-world examples:
Anthropic: Claude shows uncertainty levels. OpenAI: ChatGPT cites sources in responses. Google: Bard (now Gemini) has "double-check response" feature.
Common pitfalls to avoid:
  • Over-promising capabilities
  • Hiding limitations in fine print
  • Infrequent updates to accuracy
  • Ignoring user feedback

Conclusion & Question and Answer Session

What to Say (Script)

What to Do (Delivery Notes)

Deep Dive (Safety Net & Background Information)

Anticipated question and answer topics:

Question: What is the cost of implementation?
Answer: Varies, but typically 15-25% of chatbot development budget. Return on investment is positive within 18 months.

Question: What about smaller companies?
Answer: Start with clear communication and honest limitations. Transparency is free.

Question: How do you measure trust?
Answer: Net Promoter Score, retention rates, usage frequency, and direct user surveys.

Key takeaway to emphasize if needed:
Trust is the new competitive advantage in AI. Companies that prioritize it will lead the market.
